### Overall Verdict
**It has real heart and potential as a warm digital heirloom for our one big family, but it is not yet fully there for a 68-year-old elder wanting to feel the "one big family umbrella" with clear, readable lineage across households.**

The live landing is genuinely moving — dark bg-[#0a0a0a] with soft glows, massive serif "Mehta Family." in gold gradient, "Transcending generations and borders," heritage cards create a respectful, almost sacred first impression that matches our values. Culture page continues that warmth. The data model (multiple households + relationships) and visualiser (React Flow nodes explicitly showing household + cross connections) support the migration story. Header/footer consistency and privacy respect (PrivacyField, public redaction, amber notes) are thoughtful.

**What feels different (and weaker) when truly running**: The theme flip is stark and repeated (dark dramatic → light clean corporate directory with bg-surface cards and muted slate text). Visualiser is a loading spinner then canvas, not immediately inviting. Public views dominated by placeholder "John Doe" test households with "No members recorded." and "—" — lineage and global connections are promised on landing but hidden or absent until login or full JS. It feels more like a professional secure directory tool than a cozy, living family heirloom you want to browse slowly with grandchildren. Readability mostly good (serif warmth, responsive) but muted contrast and truncation will tire imperfect eyes. No major crashes, but friction that static review didn't fully reveal.

With theme unified (or gentle warm mode for internals), richer demo data highlighting real cross-household/global ties, stronger visualiser accessibility, and slightly larger/contrasty text, this could become something our family treasures across borders. Right now, it respects us but doesn't fully embrace and connect us the way the landing promises.

As an elder user, I'd sign in if I could, explore the tree, and then ask my tech-savvy relatives to help fill in the real stories.
